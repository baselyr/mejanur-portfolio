Weekly Reflections

Gibbs' Reflective Model applied to Professional Practice module

Week 1 13 Jan 2026
Team Formation & Gibbs Model Introduction
Initial uncertainty about 5-person team dynamics transformed into confidence through clear role differentiation. Recognized my tendency toward technical solutioning before problem definition; consciously delayed React Native vs Flutter research until user requirements were finalized.
Week 2 20 Jan 2026
User-Centred Design & Persona Development
Interviewing "Anxious Achiever" Amy revealed disconnect between my technical assumptions and user emotional needs. This tension shaped FAP's dual anonymity approach: technical tokenization + UI design obscuring submission confirmation timing.
Week 4 03 Feb 2026
Technical Architecture & Technology Decisions
Technology deadlock emerged—two members proposed Flutter, I advocated React Native. Applied "spike solution" approach: built identical authentication prototype in both frameworks over 48 hours. React Native's 70% code reuse and WebSocket maturity proved decisive.
Week 5 10 Feb 2026
Presentation Skills & Investor Pitching
Initial presentation draft was technical specification—jargon-heavy, feature-list format. Tutor feedback: "You're selling the solution, not the problem." Restructured to lead with NHS breach statistics. Recorded rehearsal revealed 23 "um" instances; deliberate pause practice reduced to 7.
Week 9 10 Mar 2026
Self-Directed Study & CW2 Transition
CW2 isolation contrasted sharply with FAP team momentum. Implemented artificial accountability: weekly check-ins with study partner, library study sessions, Trello board for essay progress. Discovered "research" phase can mask procrastination—limited to 2 Pomodoro sessions before synthesis required.
